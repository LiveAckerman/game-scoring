## Design Context

### Users
This product serves two closely related usage patterns:

- Friends using the mini program for quick, temporary scorekeeping during gatherings, parties, and card or mahjong sessions.
- Stable groups of regular players who return over time and want lightweight long-term recordkeeping across rooms and sessions.

The interface is used in fast-paced social settings, often one-handed and under time pressure. The core job to be done is to let people create rooms, join quickly, record scores with minimal friction, and review results without interrupting the atmosphere of the game.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel festive, lively, and joyful. The intended emotional tone is:

- lively and social, like a table full of familiar friends during a holiday game
- celebratory and ritualistic, with a sense of occasion rather than neutral utility
- cheerful and upbeat, while still feeling reliable enough for repeated scorekeeping

Personality keywords:

- lively
- festive
- joyful
- upbeat

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the existing mini program visual language as the baseline: compact, touch-friendly, and information-dense enough for fast task completion, in the spirit of WeChat and Alipay mini program UI conventions.

Visually, the product should lean into a Chinese festive card-table atmosphere:

- primary palette centered on Chinese red such as `#C0392B` or `#E74C3C`
- gold accents such as `#F39C12` or `#FFD700`
- warm, rich backgrounds; prefer deep red, dark red, warm cream, or red-tinted surfaces over flat pure white screens
- references include holiday red-envelope pages, WeChat red-envelope energy, and game UI patterns from apps such as NetEase Mahjong and JJ Mahjong

Typography should balance character and usability:

- titles can be more expressive and celebratory, with a slightly calligraphic or ceremonial feel when appropriate
- body text must remain clean, readable, and fast to scan during score entry

The desired overall feeling is “playing cards during the New Year”: lively, tense, festive, and ceremonial, but never confusing. Visual energy should support the moment without slowing down interaction.

### Design Principles
- Scorekeeping speed comes first. Any visual treatment must preserve quick comprehension, fast tapping, and minimal input friction.
- Keep the atmosphere festive, not noisy. Use red, gold, gradients, and celebratory motifs with restraint so the product feels lively rather than cluttered.
- Favor touch-friendly compactness. Controls should feel dense enough for utility, but always sized and spaced for confident use inside a mini program.
- Build ritual and mood into key moments. Room creation, sharing, joining, and result review should feel a little ceremonial, not like generic form flows.
- Stay consistent with the current product language. Continue using rounded cards, warm surfaces, clear hierarchy, and mini program-native interaction patterns rather than introducing unrelated design systems.
